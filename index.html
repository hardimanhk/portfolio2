About Me

I grew up in Chesterfield, VA right outside of Richmond. I've loved art for as long as I can remember, painting in particular. I followed that path up until college when I enrolled in VCU's School of Art. After about a year of that torture I decided to move on and spent a year taking various classes without aim. Eventualy, I earned a place in the biomedical engineering program and graduated with my bachelors several years later.

Still unable to figure out what to do with myself I decided to get my masters degree. I heard of a program through The da Vinci Center at VCU which endeavored to harness the power of crossfunctional teams. After successfully completing that degree I was no closer to figuring out what to do with my life. Logically, I quit my partime job, adopted a puppy and drove cross-country.

Upon my return to Richmond things began to fall into palce. I got my first "real job" and I started to think about my career in earnest. It was during this time I legitimately considered taking classes in web development. Now I am enrolled in a coding bootcamp at University of Richmond and I couldn't be happier. Coding is something I am truly passionate about and I am eager to start my career in that field.
